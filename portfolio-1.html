Gulf Of Mexico Research

Conference Poster

Project Details

The Gulf of Mexico plays a crucial role in influencing precipitation patterns across the United States (U.S.) by acting as a warm-moist reservoir, supplying essential moisture and warm air to the inland regions. However, the precise quantitative impact of the Gulf of Mexico on U.S. rainfall remains unexplored. Hence, we ask, "to what extent does the presence of the Gulf of Mexico impact U.S. precipitation patterns?" This is the question I have been working on with the Extreme Weather Laboratory under Dr. Daniel Chavas for the last 4 semesters. To quantify the Gulf Of Mexicoâ€™s influence, we simulated a CAM5 global climate model with the Gulf of Mexico filled in with grasslands. We then rigorously analyzed the generated data against real-earth historical control data. After initial cleaning and processing, we first employed cluster analysis to categorize the dataset based on distinct seasons and regions, allowing us to discern seasonal and regional variations in the Gulf's influence on precipitation. Next, we generated timeseries charts to scrutinize precipitation patterns over distinct time periods, contrasting the experiment and control data and looking for statistically significant variations. Finally, to discern potential contributing factors, we conducted correlation analysis to ascertain relationships between precipitation changes and variables such as wind speeds, moisture content, and temperature. This project served as my Honors thesis and I presented my findings at the Fall Expo in 2022. We are now in the process of compiling our findings into a research paper to be published. Participating in this project as an undergraduate researcher has been immensely fulfilling. It provided me with hands-on experience in data analysis and simulations, as well as deeper appreciation for the interdisciplinary nature of scientific research. Being able to apply computer science skills to advancing our understanding of fundamental climate dynamics has been profoundly rewarding, reaffirming my passion for research and driving my aspirations in the field.
